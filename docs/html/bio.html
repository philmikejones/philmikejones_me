Bio¶

About¶

I write code to analyse spatial and non–spatial data sets. I release as much as I can under open–source licenses. I am the author of rakeR, an R package for simulating spatial data. You can see some of my other code projects on GitHub.

Education¶

I have a PhD in human geography from the University of Sheffield (2018). My thesis is, ‘A Spatial Microsimulation Analysis of Health Inequalities and Health Resilience’. You can read the manuscript or view the source code. I was supervised by Professors Dimitris Ballas and Liddy Goyder, with support from Doncaster Metropolitan Borough Council, especially Laurie Mott.

I have an MA with distinction in social research from the University of York (2010), and a BA (Hons) in Sociology from the University of York (2005).

Current role¶

I am currently a lecturer in policing (research methods) at the University of Derby in the Department of Criminology and Social Sciences. I previously worked on the “Long-term impacts of ‘Thatcherism’: crime, politics and inequality” project.
